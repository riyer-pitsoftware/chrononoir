# Blended_Product_Plan.md

## 1. Executive Summary
We will launch **ChronoNoir Studio**, a blended product that combines cinematic storyboarding (Neo-Mumbai Noir) with auditable, historically grounded portrait generation (ChronoCanvas).  
The v1 target is two ICPs: creative educators and visual storytellers who need fast, believable character visuals with accountability.  
The core customer promise: create recognizable, context-accurate visual narratives in minutes, not days.  
Our commercial edge is a dual value stack: emotional creativity (better stories) plus institutional trust (traceable outputs).  
We will win by packaging a simple self-serve creator plan and a team plan for schools/studios with review workflows and export-ready outputs.  
The near-term revenue motion is hybrid PLG + light sales for education/studio teams.  
The immediate change now: reposition from “AI pipeline demo” to “outcome product” with clear buyer language, pricing, and launch sequencing.  
Success in 90 days is defined by paid conversion from active creators, repeat weekly usage, and pilot expansion in education teams.  

## 2. Market & Customer
**ICP 1 (B2C Prosumer):** Indie storytellers, comic creators, game writers, and concept artists creating character-consistent scenes quickly.  
**ICP 2 (B2B Team):** Educators, learning designers, museum/heritage program teams, and small creative studios needing explainable visual outputs.

**Buyer vs User**  
B2C: buyer=user (individual creator).  
B2B: buyer is department lead/program manager; users are teachers, content designers, and creative contributors.

**Top JTBD**  
- “Help me turn text ideas into compelling, character-consistent visual sequences.”  
- “Help me generate historically plausible portraits I can defend in class/review settings.”  
- “Help me personalize content with familiar faces while preserving contextual realism.”

**Pains**  
- Fragmented tools and manual handoffs across ideation, image generation, and refinement.  
- Low trust in generated outputs when provenance and rationale are unclear.  
- Slow iteration when teams need review, approval, and revisions.

**Gains**  
- Faster concept-to-storyboard cycle time.  
- Higher confidence in using generated visuals in educational or client-facing settings.  
- Better audience engagement through recognizable, personalized imagery.

**B2C vs B2B split (v1 assumption):** 70% B2C usage volume, 30% B2B revenue contribution from larger contract values. *(Confidence: Med)*

## 3. Competitive Landscape
**Direct competitors**  
- AI image tools with character consistency workflows  
- Education-oriented historical visualization tools  
- Generic “text-to-image + editing” creative suites

**Substitutes**  
- Manual workflow across separate generation/editing apps  
- Agency/freelancer concept art services  
- “Do nothing”: text-only lessons, static stock images

**Differentiation**  
- End-to-end narrative visual workflow, not one-off image generation  
- Traceability and reviewability as a built-in trust layer  
- Personalization path (familiar faces) that increases engagement in teaching/story contexts

**Why now**  
- Creators and educators now expect rapid visual production with AI assistance  
- Procurement and institutional adoption increasingly require explainability and risk controls  
- Willingness to pay is shifting from “image novelty” to “reliable output workflows”

## 4. Blending Decision
**Option A: Suite (single brand, modular journeys)**  
One product with two core modes: Storyboard Studio and Historical Portrait Studio.

**Option B: Platform + App**  
Core trust/audit platform with separate creator app experiences.

**Option C: Wedge + Expansion**  
Start with education portrait use case, later expand into full narrative storyboarding.

**Chosen approach: Option A (Suite)**  
Rationale: fastest path to monetization, easiest positioning (“from text to trustworthy visuals”), and strongest cross-sell between creator and educator segments.

**Tradeoffs**  
- Pros: shared brand, shared acquisition funnel, higher perceived value in paid tiers  
- Cons: broader onboarding scope, risk of muddled first-time experience  
- Mitigation: role-based onboarding paths at first-run

## 5. Product Narrative
**Positioning statement**  
For educators and visual storytellers, ChronoNoir Studio is a trustworthy visual narrative workspace that turns text into character-consistent, context-grounded imagery, unlike generic image generators, because every output includes explainable provenance and review-ready evidence.

**Messaging pillars**  
- Create cinematic visuals from plain text, fast.  
- Keep character identity consistent across scenes.  
- Generate context-grounded portraits with confidence.  
- Share outputs stakeholders can review and approve.  
- Personalize responsibly for stronger audience engagement.

**Top objections and responses**  
- “AI images are unreliable.” → We provide built-in evidence trails and quality scoring for each output.  
- “This will take too much setup.” → Guided role-based templates reduce time-to-first-result.  
- “Can we justify paying?” → Replaces multi-tool workflows, cuts production hours, and improves reusability of visual assets.  
- “Is this safe for classroom/team use?” → Includes usage guardrails, consent framing, and review checkpoints.

## 6. Packaging & Pricing
**B2C tiers**  
- **Free:** limited monthly generations, watermark, community templates  
- **Plus ($19-$29/month):** higher generation volume, faster queue, full export options  
- **Pro ($49-$79/month):** advanced consistency controls, reusable style kits, no watermark

**B2B tiers**  
- **Starter Team ($199-$399/month):** up to 10 seats, shared projects, approval flows, usage dashboard  
- **Team ($499-$999/month):** 25-50 seats, role permissions, branded exports, priority support  
- **Enterprise (custom):** larger seat bundles, procurement support, compliance reviews, onboarding services

**Value metric**  
Primary: monthly generation credits (output volume).  
Secondary: seats for B2B collaboration.

**Willingness-to-pay drivers**  
- Time saved per usable visual asset  
- Engagement lift in classrooms/audiences  
- Reduction in rework from poor consistency or untrusted outputs

**Pricing experiments**  
- Experiment 1: credit-bundle vs unlimited-fair-use on Plus tier to optimize conversion and margin  
- Experiment 2: per-seat pricing vs seat+credit hybrid on Starter Team to maximize expansion revenue

## 7. GTM Plan
**Motion:** Hybrid PLG + light sales-assisted conversion for B2B teams.

**Channel strategy**  
- Acquisition: creator content, educator communities, partner demos, template marketplace hooks  
- Activation: guided first project with “publishable in 15 minutes” milestone  
- Retention loops: reusable character libraries, recurring lesson/story packs, team review cycles  
- Expansion: move active individual users into team workspaces

**Launch phases**  
- **Alpha (Weeks 1-4):** invited creators + 3-5 educator cohorts; validate onboarding and pricing signals  
- **Beta (Weeks 5-8):** open waitlist, publish outcomes, run partner-led workshops  
- **Public (Weeks 9-12):** self-serve launch + targeted outbound to schools, studios, and heritage programs

**B2B sales motion**  
- Land with a pilot (single course/program/team)  
- Prove engagement and production-time savings in 30 days  
- Expand to multi-team annual agreement

**Partnerships**  
- Education content networks  
- Creative tooling communities  
- Heritage and museum learning programs

## 8. MVP Definition (Outcome-based)
**Top user journeys (v1)**  
- Turn a written character/story prompt into a multi-image storyboard draft  
- Create a historically grounded portrait from a text brief  
- Personalize a portrait with an uploaded reference face  
- Share a review-ready output with evidence trail and export package  
- Reuse a prior character/look across a new project

**Definition of done (business terms)**  
- New user reaches first “shareable output” in under 20 minutes  
- At least 40% of activated users generate a second project within 7 days  
- Pilot teams report measurable time savings vs prior process  
- Users can clearly explain why an output is acceptable for their use case

**Explicitly out of scope (v1)**  
- Video generation workflows  
- Large enterprise-wide governance programs  
- Broad marketplace or third-party monetization layers

## 9. Metrics & Targets
**North Star Metric**  
- **Weekly Shareable Visual Outputs (WSVO):** number of outputs exported/shared after review

**Supporting metrics (AARRR)**  
- Acquisition: visitor-to-signup rate target 8-15%  
- Activation: signup-to-first-shareable-output target 30-45%  
- Retention: week-4 retained creators target 25-35%  
- Monetization: free-to-paid conversion target 4-8% (B2C), pilot-to-paid target 20-35% (B2B)  
- Referral: invite/share rate target 15-25% of active users

**Leading indicators**  
- Median time to first output  
- Second-project rate within 7 days  
- Pilot stakeholder satisfaction score

**Guardrails**  
- Content safety incident rate below defined threshold  
- Support tickets per 100 active users  
- Output acceptance rate (not rejected by user/team)

## 10. Risks & Assumptions
**Assumptions with confidence**  
- Creators will pay for consistency + speed, not just novelty. *(Confidence: Med)*  
- Educators will pay for explainable visual outputs tied to engagement outcomes. *(Confidence: Med)*  
- Hybrid PLG + pilot-led sales can scale without heavy field-sales cost in year 1. *(Confidence: Low)*  
- Face-personalized outputs materially improve engagement and sharing. *(Confidence: Med)*  
- Legal/policy friction is manageable with clear consent and usage controls. *(Confidence: Low)*

**Key risks**  
- Ethical misuse concerns can slow adoption in institutions  
- Message confusion between “creative tool” and “trusted workflow product”  
- Unclear value metric could reduce pricing power  
- Competitive pressure from broader creative suites

**Validation plan (test first)**  
- Run 10-15 paid discovery interviews across educators and indie creators  
- Execute 3 design-partner pilots with explicit before/after productivity baselines  
- A/B test onboarding promise: “cinematic speed” vs “trusted outputs”  
- Pilot two pricing pages to validate willingness-to-pay and plan fit
